Werewolf Overview

The werewolf is a classic shapeshifting monster found in many European legends. It represents loss of control, hidden violence, and the terrifying idea that a normal person can become a beast. In horror stories, the transformation is usually painful, uncontrollable, and connected to the full moon or a curse.

Transformation

Werewolves are often ordinary humans until the curse takes over. During transformation, the body changes into a powerful wolf-like creature with claws, fangs, heightened senses, and extreme aggression.

Signs of a Werewolf

Common warning signs include missing memories after full moons, claw marks, animal-like behavior, unexplained injuries, sudden rage, and fear of silver or wolfsbane.
